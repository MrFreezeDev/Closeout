Things people ask

Honest answers.

How does the new "No Tax on Tips" law affect me?

Big deal. The OBBBA, signed July 2025, lets eligible tipped workers deduct up to $25,000 in qualified tips from federal income tax through 2028. The average refund is ~$1,400. But: you have to track your tips correctly — voluntary tips count, mandatory service charges don't. You also still owe FICA (7.65%) and state taxes. Closeout tracks the right things so you can claim the maximum deduction and not miss out.

Do I have to connect my bank account?

No. Never required. Manual entry works forever. Bank connection is optional for the auto-savings feature in Pro — and even then, we use Plaid (the same security infrastructure your bank uses).

What about cash tips I don't report?

Under "No Tax on Tips," cash tips that are reported now save you federal income tax — so there's a real financial upside to reporting accurately (the average claim is ~$1,400). Closeout doesn't report to anyone; we're a private tool. We just help you see the math both ways so you can make the call. What goes on your tax return is between you and your tax preparer.

Is this tax advice?

No — Closeout is an estimator, not a tax preparer. We help you save the right amount and export clean data. The actual filing is for you and your CPA / TurboTax / H&R Block. Same model as Mint or Hurdlr.

Which POS systems work with Closeout?

At launch: manual entry only — works for any job. In development for Q3 2026: Toast, Square, Clover, and 7shifts integrations. Email us your POS and we'll prioritize.

Why $4.99 when other tax apps cost $20+?

Because tipped workers are paid hourly + tips, not in salary. $4.99 is one drink at staff price. We don't think you should pay $200/year to know what you make.

Is there an iOS or Android app?

Web app at launch — works on every phone, no download. Native iOS app is in development based on early-user demand. Get in now to vote on what we build next.
